Max Miedinger

Swiss font-designer Max Miedinger was born 24 December 1910 in Zurich, Switzerland. Originally the font he invented was called Neue Haas Grotesk. In 1960 the font was renamed into the familiar Helvetica.

Unfortunately, there is very little information about the life of the creator of the most popular typeface without sans serifs. Between 1926 and 1930 Miedinger trained as a typesetter in Zürich, after which he attended evening classes at the Kunstgewerbeschule Zürich.




At the age of 26 he worked at Globe Advertising Studio as a printer. In 1956 Miedinger became an independent graphic designer and a year later he met Edward Hoffman in Haas. This acquaintance led to the creation of a font, which later will be referred to as Helvetica. Died Miedinger on March 8, 1980 in Switzerland.


Max Miedinger created not only Helvetica. Other his works are such fonts as Pro Arte, Horizontal, Swiss 921, Swiss 721, Monospace 821 and Miedinger.
